      # Luke and Jodie reopen GFC Muay Thai with members' help

      The Bury Times visited GFC Muay Thai in October 2024 after members helped Luke Phillips and Jodie Higson get the Bright Street Mill gym open.
  - type: markdown
    content: |
      Members gave up their own time to carry cement, push wheelbarrows and help with the building work. Jodie told the paper that they would not have done that without real respect for GFC and the people running it. Work on the kitchen was still going on when the article was published, but the training space was ready.

      Jodie also spoke about GFC's ladies-only Muay Thai sessions. She explained that women do not need to arrive fit or experienced because everyone works at their own pace. The sessions give members somewhere to train, learn practical skills and get some relief from the pressures of work and family life.

      There is a longer GFC story behind Luke's part in the article. The Bolton News had featured him in 2006, when he was nine and had just become the gym's youngest champion. Nearly 19 years later he was in the local paper again, this time running GFC with Jodie and welcoming members into a facility they had helped build.
